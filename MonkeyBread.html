Monkey Bread

Ingredients

    Dough

      
  • 1 Tsp Yeast
  • 2 Tbsp White Sugar
  • 1 1/2 Cup Warm Water
  • 3 Cups All Purpose Flour
  • 1 Tsp Salt

    • Sugar Coating

      
  • 1 Cup White Sugar
  • 1 Tbsp Cinnamon

    • Sauce

      
  • 1/2 Cup Melted Butter
  • 20g White Sugar
  • 20g Brown Sugar

Instructions

1. Make the Dough:

In a small bowl, dissolve 1 tsp yeast and 2 tbsp white sugar in 1 ½ cups warm water. Let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until it becomes foamy.

In a large bowl, mix 3 cups all-purpose flour and 1 tsp salt.

Gradually pour the yeast mixture into the flour, stirring until a dough forms.

Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.

2. Prepare the Sugar Coating:

In a bowl, mix 1 cup white sugar and 1 tbsp cinnamon.

After the dough has risen, punch it down and tear it into small pieces (about 1-inch size balls).

Roll each dough ball in the cinnamon sugar mixture to coat completely.

3. Make the Sauce:

In a small bowl, combine ½ cup melted butter, 20g white sugar, and 20g brown sugar. Stir until the sugars dissolve into the butter.

4. Assemble the Monkey Bread:

Grease a bundt pan (or loaf pan if you don’t have one).

Layer the coated dough balls into the pan.

Pour the butter-sugar sauce evenly over the top.

5. Bake:

Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

Bake for 30–35 minutes until the bread is golden brown and cooked through.

Let it cool for 5–10 minutes, then carefully flip it out onto a serving plate.

6. Serve and Enjoy:

Serve warm and pull apart the sticky, sweet pieces!
