Almond Croissant Cookies

Ingredients

    Almond filling

      
  • 1/2 cups almond flour (150g)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar (175g)
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbps unsalted butter melted (42g)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp almond extract

    • Cookie Dough

      
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter melted (226g)
  • 1 cup + 2 tbsp granulated sugar (220g)
  • ½ cup powdered sugar (50g), plus more for garnishing
  • 1 large egg (50g)
  • 1 tsp almond extract
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (390g)
  • 1 cup sliced almonds.

Instructions

1. Make the Almond Filling:

In a medium bowl, combine the almond flour and granulated sugar.

Add in the melted butter, egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract.

Stir until smooth and creamy.

Set the almond filling aside while you prepare the cookie dough.

2. Make the Cookie Dough:

In a large bowl, whisk together the melted butter, granulated sugar, and powdered sugar until fully combined and smooth.

Add in the egg and almond extract, and whisk again until the mixture is creamy.

Sprinkle the baking soda and kosher salt into the wet ingredients and stir to combine.

Gradually add the all-purpose flour, mixing until a soft dough forms.

Fold in the sliced almonds gently.

3. Assemble the Cookies:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

Scoop out a portion of cookie dough (about 2 tablespoons), flatten it slightly in your hand.

Place a small dollop of the almond filling in the center.

Carefully wrap the dough around the filling and roll it into a ball, making sure the filling is sealed inside.

Place the cookie dough balls onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving a bit of space between each one.

4. Bake the Cookies:

Bake for about 12–15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden but the centers are still soft.

Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

5. Garnish:

Once the cookies are completely cool, dust them generously with powdered sugar for that signature croissant look!
