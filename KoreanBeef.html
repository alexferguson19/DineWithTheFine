Korean Beef with Shin Ramyun

Ingredients

  • Onion
  • 350g Stir Fry Beef
  • Shin Ramen Noodles
  • 3 Garlic
  • 1 Tbsp Gochugaru
  • 1 Tbsp Gouchuchang
  • 1 Tbsp Mirin
  • 1 Tbsp Sesami Seed Oil
  • 1 Tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Instructions

1. Prepare Ingredients:

Thinly slice the onion.

Mince the 3 cloves of garlic.

Season the 350g stir fry beef lightly with salt and pepper.

2. Cook the Beef:

Heat 1 tbsp sesame seed oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.

Add the seasoned beef and cook for 2–3 minutes until browned.

Add the sliced onion and minced garlic. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes until the onions soften.

3. Build the Flavor:

Lower the heat to medium.

Stir in 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) and 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste).

Add 1 tbsp mirin (for a little sweetness) and 2 tbsp light soy sauce (you listed it twice, so we'll use two tablespoons total).

Stir everything together and cook for another minute until the sauce thickens slightly and coats the beef.

4. Prepare the Ramen:

In a separate pot, cook the Shin Ramen noodles according to the package instructions (usually about 3–4 minutes).

Drain the noodles, but keep a small amount (around ¼ cup) of noodle water to loosen the sauce if needed.

5. Combine:

Add the cooked noodles to the beef mixture.

Toss everything together, adding a splash of the reserved noodle water if it looks dry.

6. Final Touch:

Adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.

Optionally, sprinkle with extra sesame seeds or chopped green onions for garnish!

7. Serve and Enjoy:

Serve hot and enjoy your spicy, savory Korean beef ramen noodles!
