Bagels

Ingredients

      
  • 2 1/4 Cup Warm Water
    •   
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Active Dry Yeast
    •   
  • 1 Tbsp Sugar
    •   
  • 5 Cups All Purpose Flour
    •   
  • 2 Tsp Salt
  • 3 Tbsp Light Brown Sugar
  • Bagel Seasoning

Instructions

1. Activate the Yeast:

In a large bowl, combine the warm water (about 110°F/43°C), active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon sugar.

Stir gently and let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until the mixture becomes foamy. (This shows the yeast is activated.)

2. Make the Dough:

Add the flour, salt, and light brown sugar to the yeast mixture.

Mix with a wooden spoon or dough hook until a rough dough forms.

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8–10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

(If it's sticky, sprinkle in a little extra flour, but don’t overdo it.)

3. First Rise:

Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean towel or plastic wrap.

Let it rise in a warm spot for 1–1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

4. Shape the Bagels:

Punch down the dough and divide it into 8–10 equal pieces.

Roll each piece into a ball.

Poke a hole through the center of each ball with your finger, then gently stretch it into a bagel shape (about 2 inches wide in the middle).

5. Boil the Bagels:

Bring a large pot of water to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.

Carefully drop bagels into the water (2–3 at a time) and cook for about 1–2 minutes per side.

The longer you boil, the chewier the bagel!

6. Add Toppings:

Remove boiled bagels and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

While they’re still damp, sprinkle with bagel seasoning (like Everything Bagel seasoning or sesame seeds).

7. Bake:

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

Bake the bagels for 20–25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and puffed.

8. Cool and Enjoy:

Let the bagels cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before slicing.

Enjoy fresh, toasted, or however you like!
